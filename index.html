Canadian Certifed Pedothists are foot orthotic and orthopaedic footwear experts. They are one of the few heathcare professionals trained in the assessment of lower limb anatomy and muscle and joint function.

With specialized education and training in foot orthotics and footwear, Canadian Certified Pedorthists help to alleviate pain, abnormalities and debilitating conditions of the lower limbs and feet.

Fabrication Methods & Processes:

The fabrication of an orthotic begins with an impression of the patient’s feet using a non- weight bearing method and foam casting box technique (accommodative) OR prone non- weight bearing slipper casting in sub Talar- neutral. Both impression and patient- specific written instructions are sent to the lab.

The lab manufactures a wide range of custom-made foot orthotics using a variety of materials. A plaster cast is made and modified as necessary to include improved support under the arch and metatarsal heads. Polypropylene thermoplastic or carbon fiber material is used to form a shell, which is available in various thicknesses ranging from 2mm to 9mm (EVA-accommodative) for the desired level of control and dimensional stability. This material is placed over the cast where it is formed under vacuum. Using various grinding wheels and belts, the shell is then shaped to the specifications indicated. The covering layers are selected based on functionality and patient’s preference, with attention given to the specific cushioning needs of the patient. The orthoses undergoes quality assurance prior to being shipped out to the clinic/clinician and dispensed by the Professional to their patient.

Compression socks are exactly what they sound like, a snug fitting but stretchy sock that improves blood flow in the legs. They can lessen swelling and pain levels in the lower extremity and have been proven to lower your chances of getting deep vein thrombosis (DVT), a kind of blood clot.

There are different levels of compression and all have gradual compression which means that the sock is tightest at the ankle and lessens in the amount of compression moving up the leg.

They come in many different lengths depending on what the patient is prescribed from their family doctor. Knee length all the way up to pantyhose.

In order for compression socks to be covered by majority of insurance companies, a prescription from you family doctor is required before a fitting assessment can be completed.

Levels of Compression:

  • 15-20mmhg (Non-Medical Grade or Off The Shelf)*
  • 20-30mmhg (Medical Grade)
  • 30-40mmhg (Medical Grade)
  • 50-60mmhg (Medical Grade)**

* A prescription is NOT required in order to purchase.
** Only used for patients with major swelling issues or conditions (lymphedema)

Who uses compression socks?

  • Pregnant Women
  • People standing all day at work
  • People at risk of circulation problems
  • People who spend long bouts on airplanes
  • People who travel long distances in the car
